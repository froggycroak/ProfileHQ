### 摘要

走道式组合是一种常见的空间组合模式，其影响能扩展到建筑体量的形态特征，因而产生了走道式组合主导的建筑体量集合。在城市中，这类建筑体量集合普遍存在，且在形态规律和设计方法上呈现出共性，因而具有广泛的研究价值。随着计算机技术应用的普及，程序作为建筑知识载体和建筑设计工具成为可能。通过编写程序来把握一类建筑体量的形态特征，能够将模糊零散的设计知识整合到更为明确的结构中。另一方面，实际设计工作也需要高效的工作流程和合适的辅助工具，来解决与之相关规划和建筑设计问题。
本研究以大学校园中的学生公寓组团、生活区建筑群为例，研究走道式组合主导下单一地块上建筑体量集合的生成式设计。理论研究部分从空间组合模式和城市形态现象出发，依据形态类型学理论梳理走道式组合的内在要素、结构、逻辑，并由此引出两个生成实验的研究问题；在此基础上以“类型”语境的探讨回应了研究背景中的现象认知；最后从数据结构和算法范式的角度，探析了实验中可能用到的建模方式和算法工具。
方法研究部分包含两个实验。学生公寓组团生成实验以规则系统为主要范式，完成了一种学生公寓建筑组团的生成方法。该实验将常见的学生公寓走道生成概括为一系列可操作的步骤，给出了一种由地块形态“译制”走道形态的方法，同时通过引入建筑师常用的“模数”概念简化了转角处理的问题。高校生活区生成实验以数学规划为主要模型，转译了高校生活区中的要素和约束条件，借助通用解算器求解问题。该实验尝试了建筑生成设计中应用尚且有限的二次规划连续模型，给出了二次规划模型中复杂凹多边形地块，以及建筑编组这一“类语法”秩序的表述方法，这些内容尚未被解决且具有实际意义。在生成实验的基础上，研究通过应用框架组织算法模块，并在实例中检验可行性。
本研究在建筑形态认知和基于形态特征的生成设计中做出了有益尝试，为大学校园中一些实践问题提供了数字化解决方案，在学科知识转化、算法工具应用、设计生产提效等方面具有创新价值。

### Abstract

The corridor-based spatial organization is a common architectural method that significantly influences the form of building volumes. The affected building volumes exhibit common features in morphological traits and design methods. As programming has become a feasible medium for architectural knowledge and a tool for design, scattered and ambiguous design knowledge can be integrated into a more defined structure, by using algorithms. Additionally, efficient workflows and appropriate tools are necessary in addressing urban planning and architectural design problems.
This study focuses on the generative design of building volumes in a plot, specifically student dormitory clusters and university residential areas, under the domination of corridors. The theoretical research begins with an analysis of morphological phenomena, using morphological typology theory to dissect the intrinsic elements, structures, and logic of corridor-dominated building volumes. This leads to two different experiments. Finally, potential modeling methods and algorithmic tools used in the experiments are examined.
The following parts include two experiments. The first one generates student dormitory clusters using mainly a rule-based system. It summarizes the design of corridors into a series of operable steps and translates plot shape into corridor form. The second experiment uses mathematical programming as the main model, translating elements and constraints in university residential area design and solving problems with a general solver. This experiment explores the application of quadratic programming in architectural design. It introduces methods for expressing complex concave polygonal plots and grouping-rules within the quadratic programming model. Based on the experiments, algorithmic modules are organized and tested with two pipelines.
This study makes beneficial attempts in architectural morphological cognition and generative design based on morphological features. It provides digital solutions to practical problems in university campuses, demonstrating innovative value in transforming disciplinary knowledge, application of algorithmic tools, and enhancement of design productivity.
